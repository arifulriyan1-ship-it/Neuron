Section 1

What is a Neuron? 🧐

A neuron is a special cell that sends messages inside your body — kind of like a tiny messenger working for your brain!

💭 Imagine this: neurons are tiny messengers carrying signals from your brain to your body — and back again!

You have billions of them inside you right now, all passing notes faster than you can blink. They help you think, move, feel, and remember everything you do.

Section 4

Electrical & Chemical Signals ⚡🧪

Neurons actually use two kinds of "messages" to get the job done.

⚡

Inside the Neuron

The message zips along like an electrical signal — like a tiny bolt of lightning racing down a wire!

🧪

Between Neurons

Special chemicals called neurotransmitters jump across the gap to carry the message to the next neuron.

📱 Think of it like texting a friend!

📱 "Ouch, hot!" ⚡ ➡️ 📱

Your message travels through wires (electricity) and then "jumps" through the internet to your friend's phone (chemicals) — just like a neuron signal!

Section 5

Types of Neurons 🧬

Not all neurons do the same job — meet the three main types!

👀

Sensory Neurons

Carry information from your sense organs to your brain.

Example: Touching something hot 🔥
💪

Motor Neurons

Carry orders from your brain to your muscles.

Example: Moving your hand away ✋
🔗

Interneurons

Connect neurons inside your brain and spinal cord.

Example: Thinking and deciding 🤔
